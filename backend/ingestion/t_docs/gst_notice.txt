[CHUNK_ID: GST_CORE_001]
[TOPIC: GST Return Filing Ecosystem]
[SOURCE: CGST Act, GST Portal]

The GST return filing ecosystem follows a linked compliance flow. GSTR-1 captures outward supplies reported by suppliers. Data from GSTR-1 populates the recipient's GSTR-2B, which is the primary document used for Input Tax Credit (ITC) eligibility verification. Taxpayers then file GSTR-3B to declare tax liability and discharge GST dues. Annual reconciliation is performed through GSTR-9 and GSTR-9C where applicable.

Key Returns:

* GSTR-1: Outward supplies.
* GSTR-2B: Static ITC statement.
* GSTR-3B: Tax payment return.
* GSTR-9: Annual return.
* GSTR-9C: Reconciliation statement.

Important compliance rule:
GSTR-1 must be filed before GSTR-3B for the same tax period.

[CHUNK_ID: GST_CORE_002]
[TOPIC: Input Tax Credit (ITC) Eligibility and Restrictions]
[SOURCE: Section 16 and Section 17(5), CGST Act]

A registered person can claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) only when all statutory conditions are satisfied:

1. Possession of a valid tax invoice or debit note.
2. Receipt of goods or services.
3. Tax charged by the supplier has been paid to the Government.
4. The recipient has filed the applicable GST return.

Time Limit:
ITC relating to a financial year must be claimed by 30th November of the following financial year or before filing the annual return, whichever is earlier.

Blocked Credits under Section 17(5):

* Motor vehicles (except specified cases).
* Food and beverages.
* Personal consumption expenses.
* Employee travel benefits.
* Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off or distributed as gifts/free samples.

Rule 36(4):
ITC can only be claimed to the extent reflected in GSTR-2B.

[CHUNK_ID: GST_CORE_003]
[TOPIC: GST Notice Handling Framework]
[SOURCE: Sections 61, 73 and 74, CGST Act]

Common GST notices include:

ASMT-10:
Issued during scrutiny of returns when discrepancies are identified.

DRC-01B:
Generated when tax liability reported in GSTR-1 exceeds liability discharged through GSTR-3B.

Section 73 Proceedings:
Applicable where tax has not been paid, short paid, or ITC has been wrongly availed without fraud, wilful misstatement, or suppression of facts.

Section 74 Proceedings:
Applicable where fraud, wilful misstatement, or suppression of facts is alleged.

Recommended Response Workflow:

1. Identify notice type.
2. Determine section invoked.
3. Analyse tax, interest, and penalty separately.
4. Collect documentary evidence.
5. Prepare factual and legal submissions.
6. File response within prescribed timelines.

[CHUNK_ID: GST_CORE_004]
[TOPIC: GST Reconciliation Framework]
[SOURCE: GST Portal Guidance and Compliance Practice]

GST reconciliation should be performed monthly and annually.

Primary Reconciliations:

1. GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B.
2. GSTR-2B vs Purchase Register.
3. Books of Accounts vs GST Returns.

Common Causes of Mismatch:

* Timing differences.
* Supplier non-filing.
* Incorrect tax classification.
* Credit note/debit note adjustments.
* Place of supply errors.
* Reporting mistakes.

Best Practice:
Claim ITC only after the invoice appears in GSTR-2B and maintain reconciliation working papers for audit, scrutiny, and notice-response purposes.

[CHUNK_ID: GST_CORE_005]
[TOPIC: GST Compliance Risk Indicators]
[SOURCE: CBIC Scrutiny Guidance and Professional Practice]

HIGH-RISK INDICATORS:

* GSTR-1 liability significantly exceeds GSTR-3B liability.
* ITC claimed exceeds GSTR-2B.
* Repeated return filing defaults.
* Missing Rule 42 or Rule 43 reversals.
* Unreported Reverse Charge liabilities.
* Large unexplained ITC gaps.

MEDIUM-RISK INDICATORS:

* HSN mismatches.
* E-Way Bill inconsistencies.
* Frequent amendments and corrections.

LOW-RISK INDICATORS:

* Minor reporting differences.
* Isolated filing delays.

Compliance Score Parameters:

* Return filing punctuality.
* GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B variance.
* ITC reconciliation accuracy.
* Outstanding notices.
* Pending ASMT-10 responses.
* Annual return compliance.
* LUT validity (for exporters).
